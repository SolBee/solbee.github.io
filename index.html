Bio

I am a Research Professor in the Smart Computing Laboratory at Hallym University. Before joining Hallym University, I was an Assistant Researcher at the Smart Network Research Center in the Korea Electronics Technology Institute (KETI). I received my Ph.D. in Convergence Software from Hallym University, where I worked under the supervision of Dr. Eui-Jik Kim, and my B.S. in the same field, graduating with distinction from both programs. Throughout my academic journey, I have focused on wireless communications (including sensor networks, low-power and lossy networks, maritime wireless communication, and wireless powered communication networks) and applied machine learning. I have been involved in multiple collaborative research projects funded by the National Research Foundation of Korea and other national agencies, spanning topics such as secure vehicle data frameworks, wireless power transfer for IoT, and EMF safety in smart societies.
